Cinnamon Lip Balm

Ingredients:

1 tablespoon coconut oil
1 tablespoon beeswax (my grandma gave me some she had, but this can be purchased on Amazon)
2 tablespoons shea butter
8 drops Cinnamon Essential Oil Lip balm containers or chapstick tubes (I used these)

Directions:
  1. In a small pot over medium low heat melt beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter.
  2. Remove from head and add lavender and peppermint oils. Whisk well to distribute oil throughout the mixture.
  3. Pour quickly into lip balm containers or chapstick tubes. Let cool on counter til hard.




My Thoughts: I personally love this recipe. I used Lime & Bergamot Essential Oil in place of the Lavender and Mint Essential Oil. It smells great, and helps with my chapped lips during the cold winters.

Tip: If you like your lips often (like I do) add 1 teaspoon of honey to make your lip balm sweet!

Shampoo & Conditioner Recipe

This will be thicker than most recipes found online

Shampoo Ingredients:

1 1/4 cup/310ml maple water, coconut water, distilled water, purified or filtered water
1 tablespoon oat flour or NuNaturals Oat Fiber
4 tsp tapioca or arrowroot starch
1 tbsp bicarbonate of soda
8 drops pure essential oil (I used mint and lavender)

Conditioner Ingredients:

1 tbsp apple cider vinegar or coconut vinegar
2 drops rosemary essential oil
3/4 cup almond or coconut milk
1.5 tablespoon oat flour or NuNaturals Oat Fiber
1.4 tsp tapioca or arrowroot starch
8 drops pure essential oil (I used mint and lavender)

Directions:

Mix the chosen liquid, oat flour and starch in a small saucepan and place over a low-medium heat. Sitr constantly with a rubber spatual to get to the edges, until thickened to a light gel, about 5 minutes. Transfer to a jug and leave to cool before stirring in the bicarbonate of soda and essential oils and transferring to a re-purposed shampoo container.

My Thoughts: This does not lather like the normal shampoo would. It also seems to not spread well, but it does smell great. Also, on the downside they say it take at least a week for your natural oils to balance out, but I can't get past the second use. It makes my hair super greasy, which should be the natural oils in my hair. I use this once a week, and not more than once before I use regular shampoo.

Tip: I tried boiling the water for this recipe to purify it and then added it to the saucepan with the other ingridents. DO NOT do this it make the arrowroot gel up as the water is hot already and it doesn't mix like it is suppose to! Use cold water for your liquid choice.

Each recipe filled a 8oz shampoo bottle and I used waterproof labels to label them!

Laundry Soap Recipe

Ingredients:

2 cups Hot Water
1 cup baking soda
1/3 cup salt
1 cup Castile Soap
10 drops lavender essential oil
Mason Jar

Directions:

Mix all the ingredients together. Use 1/4 cup for 1 load of laundry. Shake well before each use. There is no binding agent for these ingredients and they will settle over time!

My Thoughts: First off this smells good and is relaxing. It makes my laundry room smell great also! The clothes come out smelling fresh and clean. PLUS this is way cheaper than purchasing laundry soap with all the chemicals in them!

Tip: I'd advise having a container that you can easily shake. Be sure to shake well before each use. I'd also recommend having a cup to measure how much to put into each load.
